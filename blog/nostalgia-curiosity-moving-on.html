Nostalgia, curiosity, and moving on

During my conversations with my therapist, I managed to identify and differentiate longing from nostalgia. I understood that recalling and associating moments and situations with the past is not wrong but rather natural.

Today, I felt curious, and what I saw didn't make me sad. In fact, it made a lot of sense and, to some extent, it made me happy. Happy because I realized the constancy and continuity of life.

Just as it was with me today, she continues to move forward. I dare say faithfully. The same companion that was once with me now follows the path of another love, someone who perhaps deserves her in a way I could no longer.

I pondered a lot before indulging my curiosity. Last time, it didn't do me any good. Today, I believe it was worth it. I still write without fully comprehending my feelings, but there was no shock or tears. It's been so long.

It's still challenging to separate the before and after. I try to imagine how your life is, how you've been, how you met your current partner, what your routines are like, but all I have are social media photos. Everything else is the fruit of my imagination.

I've also moved on, but I still relate many current situations to past experiences. I lack the courage to discuss this with anyone. I don't think it's right. I attempt to fathom why, even after so long, this story still resides within me.

There are few answers. What remains for me is to continue living and creating new memories to supplant the four years of blocks that I constructed with someone who no longer resides in this structure. In a way, a part of me still holds a part of her.

You've changed, but your face and expressions remain the same. That's how I realized, through the photos, that you're happy. I knew you like no one else did. Your smile and the strength in your intertwined hands convey what I once possessed.

This is the issue with the interconnected world. Everything is readily available. With a few clicks, my imagination gave way to reality. It wasn't difficult, but the image on the screen brought back four years I've been trying to leave behind.

Nevertheless, it was worth it. Now I know how you are. This was my curiosity, and I've satisfied it. From this point forward, I don't want to erase it from my memory but to create space for new moments and a future without the fear of encountering you, living.

I contemplate how common or natural my feelings are, but I strive to understand that the time we spent together was so intense that it's etched within me in a way that I won't be able to overwrite the entire story in such a short time.

Soon, I'll be thirty years old. You've turned twenty-eight. When I met you, I was so young that I wasn't your type. Now, I'm a part of your story just as you're a part of mine, and I'll move forward carrying all the growth we experienced together, attempting to apply it to my future relationships.
