Going out with a girl from Tinder

I would start this blog entry by saying, "Today was a good day," but it actually happened last Saturday. I'm still dealing with my abrupt breakup. I can't say anymore that I didn't see it coming because almost two months later, I can think about a lot of signs that we showed each other.

However, I'm not writing about that. As mentioned, Saturday was a good day. I connected with this girl on Tinder, and we chatted for about a week before arranging our in-person meeting. I suggested visiting a newly opened brewery pub in my city.

It was my second time there, but the first one with good company. That girl works in social media, but she's also really talented at writing poems. She talked a lot about her passion, read some poems to me, told stories, and we laughed a lot.

It was a cold night, and we were not dressed for it, so I invited her to take a ride around the city in my car. And there we were. She didn't stop talking, and I think this happens when people feel comfortable with the company.

We went to a quiet neighborhood, but she thought it was too calm, so I took her to a skatepark. I grabbed a blanket that I had in the car and offered it to her, and we sat by the ramp and talked about the graffiti, an owl, and many other things.

She wanted more beer, so we went to a gas station and stayed there for an hour or more. While we were there, the thought passed through my mind several times. It felt like we had already known each other for a long time.
